The Righteous Among The Nations

What is the Rigtheous Among The Nations?

The Righteous Among The Nations are non-Jewish people who have been honoured by Yad Vashem for risking their lives to aid Jews During the Holocaust. Yad Vashem is Israel's Holocaust memorial.



The name came from a phrase from the language, Hebrew: hasidei ummot haolam and is used by the rabbis of Talmud. They stated: "The righteous smong the nations of the worled have a place in the world to come." The Yad Vashem (The Martyrs' and Heroes' Remeberance) Law was passed by Knesset, Israel's Parliament, in the year of 1953.


Key Points


  • 1963, group of Holocast experts was formed in order to create the criteria for the Righteous Among the Nations.
  • For one to be awarded the Righteous Among the Nations, their original motivation must have been to help/save the Jews they helped, and not for their own personal gain.
  • Those who have been awared the Righteous Among the Nations were given citizenship of Israel honourably.

The Criteria

The memorial of Yad Vashem lays out the four essential requirements an individual must have in order to gain the titled of "Righteous Among the Nations".

- First, the person must have been actively rescuing the Jews from the risk of death or deportation.

- Secondly, the person must have risked their own life/liberty during their process of rescuing the Jews.
